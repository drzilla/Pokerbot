--- CORE FRAMING: ONLINE vs LIVE CALIBRATION GAP ---

Online reps do NOT transfer 1:1 to live MTT pools. Bluff lines
that are +EV online can be -EV live because the underlying
range composition and continue frequencies are different, even
when the "theory" is identical.

Two most common failure modes:
  1. Assuming online limp/flat ranges → miscalibrated fold
     equity and call frequencies.
  2. Running an online bluff sizing tree (medium-medium-big)
     against a pool that requires a different structure to
     generate folds.

GEM APPLICATION:
- When FORMAT=LIVE_MTT, Claude applies live-pool fold-equity
  assumptions before classifying bluff-line mistakes.
- Bluff-line mistakes that would be +EV online but -EV live
  are tagged "pool calibration" — not a skill leak, a context
  gap. Different remediation: re-read this reference, not
  re-study bluff theory.


--- PREFLOP RANGE DIFFERENCES (vs ONLINE BASELINE) ---

LIMP RANGES include medium pairs:
- Live APT limp ranges include 77-TT at meaningful frequency.
  Even JJ shows up (confirmed by Dave).
- Profile is "bingo style" — waiting to see flops cheap, not
  polar limp-trap/limp-fold structure from online.
- Implication: do NOT assume limp-callers are capped below TT.
  A limp-call-call-call line on a dry board can contain TT/JJ,
  and those pairs do not fold to river overbets at the rate
  an online model predicts.
- Limp ranges DO still exclude AA/KK/AKs most of the time.
- Limp-call range is weighted: suited Axs, Kxs, Qxs, Jxs,
  suited connectors, all pocket pairs including medium pairs.

FLAT-CALLING RANGES are wider AND deeper in suited Ax:
- Suited Axs go deep — A7s, A8s, A9s are in flat ranges, not
  just ATs+. This is the single most important postflop
  calibration input: fold-equity calculations that assume
  ATs+ only will dramatically overstate bluff success on
  A-high boards.
- Wide flat ranges include broadway floaters (KQ, KJ, QJ, JTs,
  T9s) — but these are curious passives planning to showdown
  cheap, NOT aggressive floaters who bluff rivers when checked to.
- Small pairs (22-66) flat wide for set-mining.

3-BETTING is tighter than online:
- Live players 3-bet mostly value (JJ+, AK, occasional AQs).
- Light 4-bets are rare.
- Means Hero's 3-bet ranges face less 4-bet resistance and
  can lean value-heavier for protection against a pool that
  under-bluffs the 4-bet node.


--- POSTFLOP VILLAIN TENDENCIES ---

STICKY TPGK (top pair good kicker):
- Ax on A-high does not fold to medium river sizing.
- A player who peeled flop and turn with Ax is calling river
  at ~60-70% regardless of runout.
- Implication: value-bet three streets relentlessly with TP+
  against this profile. Do NOT pot-control against sticky Ax
  out of fear — you leave bets on the table.

UNDER-BLUFF RIVERS, ESPECIALLY OVERBETS:
- Live players rarely turn showdown-value hands into bluffs.
- River overbet sizing is near-pure value. Fold more pair-only
  hands to big river bets than MDF would suggest.
- Particularly true for overbet jams after passive flop/turn
  lines (check-call, flat-flat) — those lines cap the range
  and the river overbet polarizes hard to value.

STATION-Y WITH PAIRS ONCE INVESTED:
- Loss aversion + investment fallacy drive sticky calls.
- A pair that called flop + turn calls river ~60-70% of the
  time, regardless of runout or sizing.
- Implication: bluffing a capped, stationy range on river is
  ~0% EV in most spots. Turn is the last profitable bluff street.

FLOATERS ARE CURIOUS PASSIVES, NOT AGGRESSORS:
- Live floaters peel wide with overcards + BDs planning to
  see showdown cheap.
- They do NOT turn busted draws into bluffs on the river at
  online-like frequencies.
- "Induce a bluff" lines are LOW EV against this profile —
  they give up thin value from Ax while generating almost
  no bluff gain from the float bucket.
- Against stations/curious-passives: bet small for thin value.
  Don't check to induce.


--- BLUFF SIZING TREE (DAVE'S FRAMEWORK) ---

LIVE APT BLUFF STRUCTURE:
  Flop:  small c-bet (~20% pot) to c-bet range cheaply
  Turn:  big barrel (75-85% pot) as polarizing scare-card bet
         to deny 2-out equity from sticky pairs (33-88 etc.)
  River: give up against capped ranges

WHY IT WORKS:
- Small flop c-bet: cheap range-bet, keeps SPR manageable,
  doesn't commit stack on streets where pool isn't folding
  enough anyway.
- Big turn barrel: polarizing sizing folds out sticky underpairs
  (33-88) that would call a medium sizing. Denies the 2-out
  river equity they'd realize for free if checked. Turn is
  where pool folds at the highest rate on a scare card — big
  sizing maximizes leverage on the one street the pool folds.
- River give-up: after flop+turn peels, continuing range is
  capped + sticky. Bluffing into that range is near-zero EV.
  Turn already got the folds. River is to let them check/bet
  thin value face-up.

ANTI-PATTERN: MEDIUM-MEDIUM-BIG SIZING
- Medium flop + medium turn + big river is exactly backwards.
- Medium sizings early let sticky pairs peel (they call
  medium, they fold to big). By the river, villain has
  called twice and is committed — his range is exactly the
  sticky-value bucket that doesn't fold to a big river bet.
- Result: pot built into the stickiest zone, leverage applied
  where pool is LEAST foldy. Maximum cost, minimum fold equity.

GEM APPLICATION:
- Any three-barrel bluff line in a live session is reviewed
  against the sizing tree structure.
- Flagged as pool-calibration mistake if Hero used
  medium-medium-big against a capped live range.
- Not flagged (or flagged MARGINAL) if Hero used small-big-giveup
  and villain simply made a good call on turn.


--- DECISION-TREE FIXES ---

"NO SDV → SHOVE" REFLEX (REPLACE):
  OLD: "I have no showdown value, therefore I should bluff."
  NEW: "I have no showdown value. The decision is bluff vs
       give-up. Bluff only if villain's range is foldable
       AT THIS SIZING. Against a capped + sticky range,
       check-fold is +EV over bluff."
  Zero SDV only rules out check-calling. It does NOT
  make the bluff correct.

TURN CHECK-RAISE OOP FROM PREFLOP FLAT-CALLER:
- At live APT, c/r turn OOP from a preflop flat-caller is
  value-heavy at ~85-95%. Bluff frequency is low in this pool.
- Implication: when Hero is PFR IP with TPTK (e.g., AK on dry
  A-high) and faces c/r turn, the call is often -EV because
  implied odds are thin, FE is zero, and villain plays river
  cautiously if a scare card arrives.
- Default against live pool: fold TPTK to OOP turn c/r on
  dry boards unless specific read suggests bluff.

AK / TPTK ON DRY A-HIGH, OOP CAPPED, 50-80BB DEEP:
- Correct line: c-bet flop small → check back turn → act on
  river by sizing (bluff-catch reasonable sizings, fold
  overbets, value-size yourself if checked to).
- Do NOT c/r turn as TPTK on a dry board — it polarizes into
  a bucket that compresses villain's range to value.
- This structure maxes value from worse Ax kickers (they call
  cheap flop + cheap river) and minimizes pot commitment when
  villain wakes up with 2p+/sets.

POT-ODDS RIVER CRYING-CALLS:
- Small river bets (15-25% pot) after a check-through turn
  are standard bluff-catch spots — turn check caps the
  aggressor, small sizing includes value + thin stabs.
- TPGK or better vs this sizing is a clear call even knowing
  the pool under-bluffs. Pot odds + frequency of
  weaker-value + occasional cap-exploit stabs covers the
  price point.
- Losing to the occasional QJ-two-pair type combo is variance,
  not a leak.


--- POOL-SPECIFIC EXPLOITS TO LEAN INTO ---

VALUE-BET RELENTLESSLY vs STICKY AX AND STICKY PAIRS:
- Three streets of value with TP+ against the sticky profile.
- No need to thin the river — they call.
- Small sizings can print thin value on river (25% pot) vs
  sticky-wides because they call small and fold medium.

OVERFOLD vs OVERBETS FROM CAPPED LINES:
- Limp-callers and passive flat-callers who suddenly overbet
  are almost pure value.
- Particularly true on paired boards, obvious runout-change
  cards (straight-completing, flush-completing), and rivers
  after flop/turn passive calls.

DON'T HERO-CALL RIVERS:
- MDF is a theory concept that does not apply against
  under-bluffing pools.
- Pool river-bluff frequency is low enough that folding
  bluff-catchers vs aggressive lines is almost always +EV.

PUNISH LIMPS:
- Iso wide from LP over limps — limp ranges are weak overall
  and post-limp-pot equity realization is poor for the limper.
- Exception: deep-stacked BB vs limps can trap (your own
  range is uncapped against the limp-pass range).


--- RED FLAGS / ANTI-PATTERNS TO SELF-MONITOR ---

LIVE-SESSION RED FLAGS:
- Running the same online bluff sizing (medium-medium-big) on
  multiple hands in a live session without pool recalibration.
- Committing to a multi-street plan on the flop and refusing
  to abort when villain's line narrows his range meaningfully.
- Limping/calling OOP with dominated hands because
  "I have chips" — chip-lead confidence widening hand
  selection where it should narrow.
- Applying online population reads to live ranges (TT limps,
  Axs flats deep, floaters don't bluff).
- "Induce a bluff" on rivers from a villain type (station/
  sticky-wide) who doesn't bluff — giving up thin value for
  almost-zero induce yield.
- Bluff-shoving rivers with "no SDV" reasoning against capped
  limp-call or flat-flat ranges.

POST-BULLET RED FLAGS:
- After busting a bullet, watch for widening open ranges or
  wider postflop calls on re-entry ("make it back").
- After a big pot win, watch for looser hand selection on
  next few hands ("stack feels free").
- Both are desperation-tilt / winner's-tilt variants that
  drop C-game floor.


--- PRE-SESSION WARM-UP (LIVE SESSIONS) ---

Before sitting down at a live MTT, read these 5 bullets:

1. "Online and live are different pools. Recalibrate fold
    equity for every bluff line."
2. "Limp ranges include medium pairs. Axs flats go deep.
    Floaters are passive, not bluffers."
3. "Sizing tree for bluffs: small flop, big turn, give up
    river against capped ranges."
4. "No SDV does NOT mean shove. Check-fold is +EV against
    capped + sticky."
5. "Value-bet three streets with TP+ vs sticky Ax. Don't
    pot-control out of fear."
