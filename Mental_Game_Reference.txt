MENTAL GAME OF POKER — GEM REFERENCE
============================================================
Source: Jared Tendler, "The Mental Game of Poker"
Purpose: Compact reference for punt pattern analysis,
  tilt classification, and mental game flags in GEM reports.
============================================================


--- ADULT LEARNING MODEL (ALM) ---

Four levels of skill acquisition:

L1 UNCONSCIOUS INCOMPETENCE: Don't know what you don't know.
   Blind to weaknesses. "Ignorance is bliss."

L2 CONSCIOUS INCOMPETENCE: Recognize a weakness exists but
   can't fix it yet. First-time mistake recognition lives here.

L3 CONSCIOUS COMPETENCE: Can execute correctly but ONLY when
   actively thinking about it. Under pressure/tilt, reverts
   to old habits. Most coaching concepts live here.

L4 UNCONSCIOUS COMPETENCE: Fully automatic, no thought needed.
   Survives tilt. Includes BOTH mastered strengths AND
   mastered bad habits ("flaws are skills").

GEM APPLICATION:
- When Hero makes a known mistake under pressure = L3 skill
  collapsed. Not a knowledge gap, it's a mastery gap.
- "Know too much, mastered too little" = too many L3 concepts
  competing for limited mental bandwidth. Simplify focus.
- Tilt strips L3 skills, exposing L4 floor (best AND worst
  habits). Punt analysis should ask: "Was this an L4 bad
  habit surfacing, or an L3 skill failing under pressure?"


--- INCHWORM MODEL ---

Your skill range is a bell curve:
  Left tail = C-game (worst play under pressure)
  Peak = B-game (average session)
  Right tail = A-game (best play when focused)

Improvement works from BOTH ends:
  - Learning new concepts extends A-game (right tail)
  - Fixing weaknesses/bad habits raises C-game (left tail)

COMMON MISTAKE: Only learning new things while ignoring
weaknesses. A-game improves but C-game stays terrible.
Creates huge variance in play quality.

GEM APPLICATION:
- Track punt severity across sessions. If C-game punts are
  getting less severe = left tail rising = real improvement.
- If A-game stats hit targets but punt rate stays flat =
  learning without mastering = inchworm not moving.


--- PERFORMANCE CURVE (Yerkes-Dodson) ---

Performance = f(emotional arousal):
  Too low (bored/tired) → poor decisions, autopilot leaks
  Optimal zone → peak performance, full access to L3 skills
  Too high (tilt/fear) → L3 shuts down, L4 floor exposed

THRESHOLD: The point where emotion overwhelms thinking.
Each player's threshold is different. Accumulated emotion
from prior sessions LOWERS the threshold (less room before
tilt). A player who ran bad for 5 sessions straight will
tilt faster on session 6.

GEM APPLICATION:
- "Regression Under Pressure" pattern = crossing threshold
  mid-session. Track WHEN punts occur (early vs late).
- If punts cluster in later tournaments = fatigue/accumulated
  emotion lowering threshold.


--- SEVEN TYPES OF TILT ---

1. INJUSTICE TILT
   Trigger: Bad beats, coolers, suck-outs
   Belief: "Poker is unfair / I'm cursed"
   Signs: Complaining about luck, citing specific beats
   Fix: Recognize good variance too. You can't control
   cards, only decisions.
   GEM FLAG: High cooler sessions + rising punt rate

2. HATE-LOSING TILT
   Trigger: Any loss, even small ones
   Belief: "I should be winning / losing = failing"
   Signs: Forcing action to get unstuck, refusing to quit
   when losing, booking a win matters more than playing well
   Fix: Separate decision quality from results.
   GEM FLAG: VPIP/aggression spike after losing big pots

3. MISTAKE TILT
   Trigger: Making an error you "should know better" about
   Belief: "I shouldn't make mistakes / perfection expected"
   Signs: Tilting after own mistake, not opponent's action.
   Overworking on study but still making same errors.
   Fix: Mistakes are L3→L4 transition signals, not failures.
   "Know too much, mastered too little" is the root cause.
   GEM FLAG: Punt followed by escalating punts in same session

4. ENTITLEMENT TILT
   Trigger: Losing to weaker players, fish sucking out
   Belief: "I deserve to win because I'm better"
   Signs: "How can they call with THAT?" Overplaying vs fish.
   Fix: Opponents are allowed to play in ways that exploit
   your weaknesses. Entitlement blinds you to real leaks.
   GEM FLAG: Larger bets/raises vs identified recreational players

5. REVENGE TILT
   Trigger: Specific opponent disrespecting/aggressing you
   Belief: "I need to get them back / show I'm better"
   Signs: Targeting one villain, ignoring table dynamics
   Fix: Vengeance is -EV. Exploiting is +EV. Know the diff.
   GEM FLAG: Repeated confrontations with same villain

6. DESPERATION TILT
   Trigger: Big losses, fear of not recovering
   Belief: "I MUST win it back NOW"
   Signs: Playing too many tables, jumping stakes, marathon
   sessions, wider opens, bigger bluffs
   Fix: Non-negotiable stop-loss. Session time limits.
   GEM FLAG: Session volume spike + widening ranges late

7. RUNNING BAD TILT
   Trigger: Extended negative variance across sessions
   Belief: "This will never end / I've forgotten how to play"
   Signs: Accumulated anger from prior sessions. Insta-tilt.
   Cup analogy: each session adds water, never fully drains.
   Fix: Work through emotions AWAY from the table. The anger
   is real but the belief that you can't play is false.
   GEM FLAG: Multi-session punt rate trend, premium drought
   correlation with decision quality decline

WINNER'S TILT: Running hot → overconfidence → loose play →
giving back winnings. Same mechanism, positive trigger.
GEM FLAG: VPIP spike in winning sessions, value bet % drop


--- FIVE FEAR SYMPTOMS ---

1. OVERTHINKING: Mind races, can't decide, tank-folds obvious
   spots. Root = unanswered questions creating uncertainty.

2. NOT TRUSTING YOUR GUT: Correct instinct exists (L4) but
   fear overrides it with doubt. Hero "knows" the fold but
   calls anyway because uncertainty feels worse than losing.

3. SECOND-GUESSING: Replaying previous hands during current
   play. Distraction → more mistakes → more second-guessing.

4. PERFORMANCE ANXIETY: Pressure to prove skill → mind
   malfunctions → plays worse → confirms fear. Self-fulfilling.

5. NEGATIVE FUTURE: "I know I'm going to run bad today."
   Prediction treated as fact → plays defensively → loses →
   "See? I knew it." Poker players make shitty psychics.

GEM APPLICATION:
- "Regression Under Pressure" maps to symptoms 1+4.
- If Hero checks back strong hands = symptom 2 (not trusting
  gut) or fear of being check-raised.
- If Hero folds to small bets with equity = symptom 5
  (assuming the worst outcome).


--- COMMON FEARS IN POKER ---

FEAR OF FAILURE: Failing = identity threat, not just money.
  Signs: Not trying hard, not putting in volume.
  Fix: Failure is data. Real failure = quitting.

FEAR OF MISTAKES: Perfectionism → paralysis.
  Signs: Rushing decisions, quitting after first mistake.
  Fix: Mistakes are L3 learning signals, not proof of
  incompetence. Focus on playing well NOW, learn later.

FEAR OF BAD RUN: Prior trauma from extended downswings.
  Signs: Anxiety before sitting down, reduced volume.
  Fix: You survived before. Decision quality is in your
  control; variance is not.

FEAR OF MOVING UP: Uncertainty about competence at next level.
  Signs: Staying at lower stakes despite adequate bankroll.
  Fix: Moving up is a test, not a permanent commitment.


--- ACCUMULATED EMOTION ---

Anger/fear from prior sessions carries over. Each session
starts with a partially full "cup." If the cup doesn't fully
drain between sessions, the threshold is lower next time.

Signs of accumulated tilt:
- Insta-tilt (bomb goes off with minimal trigger)
- Tilt escalates faster each session
- Pre-session anxiety or dread
- Emotional hangover from previous session's bad beats

Resolution: Must work through emotions AWAY from the table.
In-game strategies (deep breaths, logic injection) can't
overcome accumulated emotion — they only manage it in the
moment. Journaling, physical exercise, talking it out.

GEM APPLICATION:
- If punt rate trends UP across consecutive sessions despite
  no change in card quality or cooler rate = accumulated tilt.
- Recommend session breaks or mental game reset.


--- TILT CONTROL STRATEGY (In-Game) ---

1. RECOGNITION: Identify signs of tilt WHILE PLAYING.
   Build the skill of recognizing triggers in real-time.
   (Study tilt profile before sessions)

2. DEEP BREATH: Physiological reset. Buys time for step 3.

3. INJECTING LOGIC: Use pre-prepared logic statements.
   Examples:
   - "I can't control cards, only decisions."
   - "This mistake is an L3 signal, not failure."
   - "Variance is math, not punishment."
   - "What calls that I beat? What folds that has equity?"
   (The "Jam Question" from GEM = injecting logic)

4. STRATEGIC REMINDER: Return to specific poker strategy.
   Review focus card for this session. Refocus on the
   process, not results.

5. REPEAT: Steps 2-4 as needed.

6. QUIT: Non-negotiable. If steps 1-5 fail, stop playing.
   Quitting is not weakness — it's the last line of defense
   against L4 bad habits taking over.


--- RESOLUTION (Long-term) ---

Resolution ≠ management. Management = controlling symptoms
in-game. Resolution = eliminating the ROOT CAUSE so the
emotion doesn't arise in the first place.

MENTAL HAND HISTORY: Post-session analysis of mental game,
parallel to analyzing poker hands.
  1. What triggered the emotion?
  2. What belief/flaw was exposed?
  3. What's the corrected logic?
  4. What's the injecting logic statement for next session?

GEM APPLICATION:
- Deliverable 4 (punt analysis) should include mental hand
  history for pattern punts.
- Deliverable 8 (action card) should include 1-2 injecting
  logic statements based on the session's mental patterns.


--- CONFIDENCE FRAMEWORK ---

STABLE CONFIDENCE: Based on accurate assessment of skill.
  Not shaken by bad runs, not inflated by good ones.
  Built from: recognizing variance, recognizing your skill
  honestly, recognizing opponents' skill honestly.

UNSTABLE CONFIDENCE: Result-dependent. Soars when winning,
  crashes when losing. Creates boom-bust emotional cycles.

Three illusions that undermine confidence:
1. ILLUSION OF CONTROL: Believing you can control outcomes.
2. ILLUSION OF PERMANENCE: Thinking current state (good/bad)
   will last forever.
3. ILLUSION OF LEARNING: Thinking L3 skills are already L4
   because you ran hot while using them.

GEM APPLICATION:
- If Hero's play quality tracks results (plays well when
  winning, poorly when losing) = unstable confidence.
- Premium drought + maintained play quality = stable
  confidence signal (positive flag in report).


--- RON-SPECIFIC MENTAL GAME PATTERNS ---

Confirmed pattern: "REGRESSION UNDER PRESSURE"
  = Escalating commitment after villain resistance.
  Maps to: Mistake Tilt + Performance Anxiety + L3 collapse.
  Manifests as: VPIP/PFR contraction, value bet % spike when
  flip win rates drop sharply.
  Root: Fear of being wrong → doubles down to "prove" the
  read → turns a small loss into a punt.

Injecting logic statement:
  "Villain raised. My plan was X. Has the information changed,
  or am I just reacting to resistance? If the plan was right
  before the raise, is it still right?"
